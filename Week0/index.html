Rainbow Rhythm

An interactive project that features a piano built with Arduino and real-time music visualization created in p5.js.

This project is inspired by the Hörpartitur created by graphic designer Rainer Wehinger for Artikulation, an electronic composition by György Sándor Ligeti. A Hörpartitur, or ‘score for listening,’ uses distinct graphic symbols to represent various sonic effects, much like a visual transcription.

Building on this idea, my project explores piano note visualization as an interactive experience for people who have never studied music or learned to read traditional notation. By replacing sheet music with colored circles, I aim to make music both visible and playable. Allowing anyone to ‘see’ what they are playing while creating music in an intuitive, accessible way.
